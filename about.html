About Leo

Calm systems judgment, learned the long way.

I am a Silicon Valley technology operator and writer with a long memory for how systems actually fail: not only through bad code, but through rushed incentives, unclear ownership, fragile assumptions, and dashboards nobody reads until the room gets warm.

My background sits at the intersection of enterprise architecture, AI platforms, cloud operations, security posture, and product delivery. I have worked with teams that needed architecture to be elegant, but also boring enough to survive Monday morning.

My public writing is intentionally quiet. I am not trying to win the loudest headline. I am trying to understand what changed, what did not change, who is exposed, and what a serious operator should do next.

Technology lens

Architecture over fashion

I care about AI, cloud, data, and security when they become reliable leverage for real organizations.

Market lens

Mechanisms over mood

I read rates, liquidity, earnings quality, incentives, and risk transfer before I read the daily temperature of the room.

Writing style

Serious, not joyless

I keep the prose professional and restrained, with enough dry humor to admit that production systems have personalities.
