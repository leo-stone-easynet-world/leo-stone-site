About Leo

I write about technology, markets, and the work behind them.

I live in Silicon Valley and spend most of my time around technology, markets, and the practical decisions that connect the two. This site is where I keep notes on what I am reading, what feels durable, and what may be getting more attention than it deserves.

I am especially interested in AI, software infrastructure, cloud platforms, security, liquidity, incentives, and company behavior under pressure. I try to stay close to the mechanics: what changed, who benefits, where the cost moves, and what breaks if the story is wrong.

The writing here is meant to be calm and useful. I do not want every headline to become a forecast or every new product to become a revolution. I would rather keep a clear record of what matters, revise when the facts change, and leave out the noise when I can.
